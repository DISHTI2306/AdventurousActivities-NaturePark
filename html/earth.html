Colored Earth


Mauritius was created due to continental drifts and collisions, volcanic eruptions and other events which eventually led to the creation of tropical reefs and oceans, lagoons, mountains and basalt cliffs.

D&D Park represents a diverse and unique colored land with a rich cultural and natural history.

The colored earth is a natural, unsual and curious phenomenon as well as a tourist attraction.

Throughout the state, it offers a range of interpretive programs that involve visitors of all ages in experiences that promote knowledge and appreciation of the park's natural resources.

It is nearly a small area of sand hills, consisting of five distinct colors namely brown, red, blue, green and yellow. The different colors were formed through the conversion of basaltic lava to clay minerals, and also due to different composition.

Furthermore, a fascinating feature about the colored earth is that when all five separate colors are combined together, the different colored sand settles in various distinct strata as a result of the broken rocks.
In addition, each sand dot usually returns to its original color caste.

On the premises, you will also find a children's playground and some giant tortoise.
